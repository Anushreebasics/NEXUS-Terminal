# 📘 NEXUS Terminal — User Guide

If you are a trader or market enthusiast, this guide will help you navigate the NEXUS Terminal and understand the institutional-grade data at your fingertips.

---

## 🚦 1. The Dashboard at a Glance

When you first open the terminal, you'll see a high-density "mission control" for crypto markets.

- **Status Bar (Top Right)**: Look for the green **LIVE** indicator. This means you are receiving millisecond-accurate data directly from the exchanges.
- **Sentiment Gauge (Top Left)**: This tells you the "mood" of the market (Fear vs Greed). It's powered by an AI that read the last 15-20 global headlines and scored them.
- **Paper PnL (Top Center)**: This is your "Simulated Profit." The terminal automatically "trades" every time it finds a guaranteed profit opportunity between two exchanges.

---

## 📊 2. Reading the Market Matrix

The **Live Market Matrix** is the heart of the terminal. It compares prices for **BTC**, **ETH**, and **SOL** across three different venues: **Binance**, **Coinbase**, and **Uniswap**.

- **Pricing Discrepancies**: In the crypto world, one exchange might be selling Bitcoin for $65,000 while another is selling it for $65,100.
- **The Spread column**: This shows the percentage difference.
- **Green Spreads**: When the spread turns green and exceeds 0.3%, the terminal flags it as an **Arbitrage Opportunity**. This means you could (in theory) buy on the cheaper exchange and sell on the more expensive one for a risk-free profit.

---

## 📈 3. Analyzing "Market Depth" (Order Book)

In the center of the screen, you'll see the **Order Book** for the selected asset (e.g., BTC/USDT).

- **Bids (Green/Blue)**: People who want to BUY.
- **Asks (Red)**: People who want to SELL.
- **Visual Volume Bars**: The longer the bar, the more money is waiting at that price level. If you see a massive red bar, it's a "sell wall"—the price might struggle to go higher until that wall is eaten.

---

## 🔔 4. Understanding Alerts

On the bottom right, you'll see the **Detection Engine Alerts**.

1.  **Arbitrage**: A price gap was found between two exchanges.
2.  **Anomaly**: An exchange's price suddenly moved significantly away from the "global average." This could indicate a major whale trade or a technical glitch you can exploit.
3.  **Spike**: A sudden, rapid movement (up or down) in under 30 seconds.

---

## ⚙️ 5. Personalizing Your Terminal

Click the **Gear Icon** in the top right corner.

- If the terminal is "too noisy" for you, move the **Arbitrage Threshold** slider higher (e.g., to 1.0%). You will only get alerts for the biggest, most profitable gaps.
- If you want to detect even the tiniest market "shiver," move the **Anomaly** slider lower.

---

## 💹 6. Your Daily Workflow

A typical session with the NEXUS Terminal looks like this:

1.  **Check Sentiment**: Is the market in "Extreme Greed"? Be careful of a sudden drop.
2.  **Monitor the Matrix**: Look for green spreads.
3.  **Validate with Depth**: If you see a 0.5% arbitrage on BTC, check the **Order Book**. Is there enough volume at that price to actually make the trade?
4.  **Review Profits**: Check your **Paper PnL** at the end of the day to see how much "simulated money" the terminal made by automatically identifying these gaps.
